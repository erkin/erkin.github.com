Summary

Arf is my programming language idea, proposal and plan. Its syntax is based on M-expressions. Its semantics are "kinda" object-oriented, and it is strongly and statically typed. It is named after the mathematician Cahit Arf and is not related to the onomatop≈ìia of the dog's cry, nor is it an acronym.

Syntax

Defining values:

include(mathstypes)
## Functions are separated from variables with the format of name(argument)
a[int](3)
## Every variable follows the format of name[type](value) Every variable must have a value.
## There are plenty of pre-defined types (some include others, for example "num" includes "int") but it is also easy to define a new type.
b[coord[2]](12,~3)
## Types such as coordinates can take more than one value.
## Types can have types too. In this case, "coord" has a type of "2", indicating it is two dimensional.
## Type-types are obviously optional, the parser can recognise it in most cases.
## "~" indicates a negative value, to avoid confusion with "-" the numeric operation.
## (Though this is not mandatory as the plane can have varying number of dimensions if the type-type is not set.)
